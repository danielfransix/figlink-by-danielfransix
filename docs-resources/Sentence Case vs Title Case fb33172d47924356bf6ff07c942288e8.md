# Sentence Case vs Title Case

## What's the Difference?

**Sentence case** — Capitalize only the first word and proper nouns (names of people, places, brands, etc.). Everything else is lowercase.

**Title case** — Capitalize the first word, the last word, and all "major" words. Keep small words (like *a, an, the, in, on, of, and, but, or, for*) lowercase unless they start or end the title.

---

## Sentence Case Rules

1. Capitalize the **first word** of the sentence.
2. Capitalize **proper nouns** (e.g. *Nigeria, Google, Monday*).
3. Everything else stays **lowercase**.
4. **Acronyms** stay fully capitalized — *NASA, HR, API*.

**Examples**

- How to set up your payroll engine
- A quick guide to time and attendance
- Meeting notes from the May review

---

## Title Case Rules

1. Capitalize the **first and last word** — always.
2. Capitalize all **major words** (nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, pronouns).
3. Keep **small words lowercase** — *a, an, the, and, but, or, for, in, on, at, to, of, by, with* — unless they're the first or last word.
4. Capitalize words of **4+ letters** (some style guides use this as a simple shortcut).
5. **Acronyms** stay fully capitalized — *NASA, HR, API*.

**Examples**

- How to Set Up Your Payroll Engine
- A Quick Guide to Time and Attendance
- Meeting Notes From the May Review

---

## Quick Cheat Sheet

|  | **Sentence case** | **Title case** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First word | Capitalized | Capitalized |
| Major words | Lowercase | Capitalized |
| Small words | Lowercase | Lowercase (unless first/last) |
| Proper nouns | Capitalized | Capitalized |
| Tone | Casual, conversational | Formal, polished |
| Acronyms | Always fully capitalized | Always fully capitalized |
